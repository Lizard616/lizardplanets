This amazing, but fairly common creature is a type of amphibian. It's about the size of a gecko, has four legs and no arms, but they have no tail.

{{gallery:Khrank Frog}}

They have a soft, delicate skin covered in a very thin layer of mucous, which is usually either light green, light bronze or light brown or a combination of these colors.

They live in low areas and are common. They're herbivores and their large, wide mouths, their teeth and wide tongue are ideal for eating nuts.

They're nocturnal and rely on their extra sense and taste buds to get around. They do have thin, narrow eyes, but their sight is underdeveloped.

They have an almost hidden nose and tiny, almost hidden ears. Their heads are long and narrow in comparison to their bodies.

They make sounds ranging from relatively low pitched to very high pitched and have a very small range of sounds they make to indicate discoveries, dangers and otherwise communicate with each other.

These creatures are very peaceful, but they heavily defend their personal space. They mate once or twice a year and they mate with a specificly selected partner for life. Which, with their short lifespans, is only normal.
