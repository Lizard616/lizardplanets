Matchi is like the superstar of the Jux Belt who's both fabulous and just a *tiny* bit temperamental. With a diameter of 3,100 km, Matchi isn't the biggest, but boy, does it know how to command attention. Sporting three moons—Aan (the sidekick), Fior (the baby diva), and Spre (the brawny bodyguard)—Matchi travels through space like it's walking the red carpet, even though it's basically surrounded by a dusty asteroid crowd.

The surface of Matchi is a patchwork of vibrant hues—think bruised purples, icy blues, and dusty grays. It's like an artist started painting but got interrupted halfway through. There's no atmosphere to ruin the vibe, which is good because Matchi wouldn't want any "wind" smudging its look anyway.

Its 77-hour days make it the perfect place for a long, dramatic sulk—or an equally dramatic spa day. But don't let its stylish exterior fool you. Matchi's iron-rich core and subtle magnetic field suggest it's more solid than it looks, even if it doesn't brag about it.

As for its moons, they're a wild trio. Spre's the powerhouse, sticking close to protect Matchi's ego, Fior is tiny but scrappy (because size doesn't matter when you've got attitude), and Aan just sort of hangs out, probably wondering how it ended up in this chaotic family.

All in all, Matchi is the quintessential diva of the Jux Belt—always cool, always dramatic, and always photogenic under the starry limelight.
